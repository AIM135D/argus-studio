# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want ARGUS Studio to be a welcoming, practical, and technically rigorous project. Contributors and maintainers commit to an environment free from harassment and discrimination, regardless of background, identity, experience level, or role.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and constructive.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Assume good intent while remaining open to correction.
- Share context that helps others reproduce and understand a problem.
- Respect privacy, security boundaries, and the licenses of data and software.
- Accept maintainers' decisions about scope and project safety.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, discriminatory language, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Deliberately disrupting discussions, reviews, releases, or infrastructure.
- Uploading private datasets, credentials, malware, or software without redistribution rights.
- Retaliation against someone who raises a good-faith concern.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove content and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation when behavior threatens people or the project. Reports should be made privately through the maintainer contact shown in the repository's GitHub Security page. Reports will be reviewed as confidentially and promptly as practical.

This policy applies in project repositories, issue trackers, review threads, discussions, and project-related events or communications.
